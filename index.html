The Sad Truth: The Poorer You Are, The Faster You Die.

Here is a graph plotting life expectancy against GDP per capita, using a dataset from 2018.
Click on the legends on the top left to reveal more information.

Filtering by region reveals that countries within the same continent are similarly placed along the trendline. This dovetails with the fact that countries that are geographically closer tend to have similar level of wealth.

Africa takes one extreme at the lower left of the graph, while the other extreme on the top right is taken by Europe, North America, and Oceania. Middle and South American countries are clustured nicely together in the middle. Asia shares the tendency towards the center, but is spread over a much wider range. This is perhaps not surprising considering that Asia is particularly diverse in terms of geography, language, culture, history, and more, indicating greater between-country differences than other continents.
